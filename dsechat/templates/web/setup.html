Chat Client Configuration

You can connect to the data science chat room using any client that supports XMPP.

The data science chat room is hosted by an XMPP/Jabber server. XMPP is an open chat protocol that is supported by a wide range of client programs and services.

We provide instructions for connecting to the data science chat room with Pidgin and Adium, two popular open-source chat programs. However, the setup should be similar if you want to connect with another XMPP client.

Note: Before you can connect, you must have a valid username and password.

  • Pidgin (for Windows and Linux)
  • Adium (for Mac OS X)
  • Other XMPP clients

Pidgin Setup

Pidgin is a free IM program for Windows and Linux.

After downloading and installing Pidgin, go through the following steps:

1. Add an account

If you have just installed Pidgin, you will see the Accounts screen.

You can also access this screen from Accounts > Manage Accounts.

Click the Add... button to setup your account.

2. Enter your account settings

Set the Protocol to XMPP.

Enter the Username you created earlier.

For the Domain, enter {{ xmpp_server }}.

The Resource field is optional.

Enter the Password for your chat account.

The User Options are optional.

Click the Add button when you are done.

3. Enable your account

Make sure that small checkbox next to your account is checked.

This will make Pidgin sign in using your username and password.

You can close the "Modify Accounts" window.

4. Add the chat room

Now you are connected to the chat server, but you still need to join the chat room.

On the "Buddy List" window, go to Buddies > Add Chat....

5. Enter the chat room settings

Choose your data science chat account.

For Room, enter {{ xmpp_muc_room }}.

For Server, enter {{ xmpp_muc_server }}.

You may use whatever Handle you wish, but we recommend you use your username.

You may leave the Password field empty.

Press the Add button.
